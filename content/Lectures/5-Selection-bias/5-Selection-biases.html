Lecture slides: Lecture 5.

Reading: Angrist and Pischke (2014, Ch. 1: pages 1-11).

The mantra of statisticians around the world is: Correlation ≠ causation! But why? Today you will learn how selection bias confounds simple comparisons of groups. You will also learn how the potential outcomes framework defines causality and why estimating a causal effect is so difficult. Fortunately, it is not heopeless; but that’s the story of next week. First you need to understand where the challenges lie and why the simple standard approach doesn’t work.
