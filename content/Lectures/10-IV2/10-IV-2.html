Lecture slides: Lecture 10.

Reading: Angrist and Pischke (2014, Ch. 3 Instrument Variables, Pages 123-146).

Source: Wikipedia article on IV

So far we have had a helpful but quite old-school perspective on IV: We way up the causal effect of a random instrument by the people who actually complied with the treatment intended by the instrument. But now that we have a thorough understanding of the Frisch-Waugh Theorem, we can take another perspective on IV-Regression: Two-Stage Least Squares. That is, we can identify the random variation in our treatment \(D\) that the instrument \(Z\) has induced in to it. Today you learn how.

Don’t miss out on this lecture by Joshua Angrist
